Website Evaluations

CRAP refresher: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, Proximity — popularized by Robin Williams in The Non-Designer's Design Book.

Airbnb (airbnb.com)

Airbnb’s site is clean and easy to use. It has clear pictures, simple text, and organized layouts that make searching and booking straightforward.

  • Contrast: High-quality images and bold text stand out against plenty of white space.
  • Repetition: Buttons, colors, and layouts stay the same across pages, giving a familiar feel.
  • Alignment: Content lines up neatly in grids, so the eye can move smoothly across the page.
  • Proximity: Related details (like price, rating, and location) are grouped together, making them easy to scan.

Yale School of Art (art.yale.edu)

The Yale School of Art site has a messy and confusing design. Text, images, and moving pieces are scattered around, which makes it hard for new visitors to know where to look or what to click.

  • Contrast: Bright colors and busy backgrounds fight for attention, so nothing clearly stands out.
  • Repetition: Fonts, colors, and layouts change a lot, so it feels inconsistent.
  • Alignment: Items don’t line up in a clean grid, making the page hard to follow.
  • Proximity: Things that aren’t related sit close together, so it’s not clear what belongs in each group.

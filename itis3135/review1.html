Peer Review for Sumran Nassir

Overview

I reviewed Sumran Nassir's Fast Fuels site for our ITIS 3135 client assignment.

Site link: Fast Fuels home page

I read the home page text, looked at the images, and checked links in the navigation bar.

What works well

  • The Fast Fuels name appears at the top and matches the client focus.
  • The navigation labels for Home, About, Services, Gallery, and Contact are clear.
  • The welcome heading explains that Fast Fuels is a gas station in Thomasville, NC.
  • The sentence about fuel, service, and the store gives a fast overview of what visitors receive.
  • The three photos for pumps, station exterior, and store support the description.
  • The address at the bottom helps visitors see where the station sits in town.
  • Validator links in the footer support course checks for HTML and CSS.

What needs attention

  • The word “conveinent” in the welcome text has a spelling error. I suggest changing that word to “convenient”.
  • The “Current Time” label shows a close symbol. I suggest updating this feature so a clear time display appears for visitors.
  • The home page would benefit from one short list that names fuel grades or key services.
  • The gallery area would gain clarity from short captions that explain each photo.
  • A contact prompt with a short line such as “Visit us today” or “Stop by for fuel and snacks” would support user action.

Match with project requirements

  • The site follows a clear structure with header, navigation, main content, and footer area.
  • The Fast Fuels name appears near the top of the page in a consistent way.
  • Headings move in a logical order from “Welcome to Fast Fuels” down to smaller sections.
  • Navigation links lead to the expected pages and match the labels shown.
  • Content focuses on a real client project for a local gas station and store.
  • Text speaks to drivers and customers who need fuel and everyday items.
  • Images support the station story and give visitors a sense of the place.

Stop, Start, Continue

Stop

  • Leaving the “Current Time” area without a clear time display.

Start

  • Fixing the spelling in the welcome paragraph so the message reads smoothly.
  • Adding one short list of services or fuel options on the home page.
  • Writing brief captions under each gallery photo to explain what visitors see.
  • Adding a short action line near the address to invite visitors to stop by.

Continue

  • Using a direct welcome heading that names the client site.
  • Keeping navigation simple and easy to follow for each page.
  • Including the address and validator links in the footer area.

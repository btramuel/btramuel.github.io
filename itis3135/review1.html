Peer Review for Josiah Flowers

Overview

I reviewed Josiah Flowers' project site for our ITIS 3135 client assignment.

Site link: Project home page for Josiah Flowers

I moved through the home page and the main links and I took notes on structure, writing, and layout.

What works well

  • I like that your site title with your name is clear and easy to read.
  • Your navigation bar uses simple labels that match the content on each page.
  • Your welcome text on the home page explains the purpose of the site in a direct way.
  • Short paragraphs make your content easy to read.
  • Buttons and links guide your visitor toward next steps, such as reading more details.
  • Spacing between sections keeps the page from feeling crowded.
  • Headings break the content into clear groups for visitors and for screen readers.

What needs attention

  • The home page needs at least one strong visual element, such as a main image related to the project.
  • Some sections feel short. One or two extra sentences in key sections would help visitors understand the client and goals.
  • Links in the body text would benefit from short phrases that describe what a visitor sees after a click.
  • A short note about the client or organization on the home page would help users connect with the purpose of the site.

Match with project requirements

  • The site follows a clear structure with header, navigation, main content, and footer area.
  • The project name appears near the top of the page in a consistent way.
  • Headings move in a logical order from page title to section titles.
  • Navigation links work and lead to the expected pages.
  • Content focuses on a real client project for the course.
  • Text speaks to potential users and explains what they gain from the site.
  • There is space for images or future visuals without crowding the text.

Stop, Start, Continue

Stop

  • Leaving large areas of the home page without images or visual anchors.

Start

  • Adding a main hero image or banner that reflects the project focus.
  • Writing one extra sentence in each main section to give visitors stronger context.
  • Adding a short client summary on the home page so visitors know who you support.
  • Including a simple footer line with course info or a contact link.

Continue

  • Using simple headings and short paragraphs.
  • Keeping navigation clear and consistent across pages.
  • Aligning content to a central column so visitors follow the same reading path on every page.
